Binge watch and learn more about Asian-American culture:

The Asian-American and Pacific Islander experience is unique and that experience has been brilliantly captured on television. As you watch these television programs, you should pay attention to the nuances between Asian and American cultures. Those nuances are the essence of the Asian-American identity. Here are some of our recommendations.

Crash Landing on You

Crash Landing on You is a Korean telenovela about two star-crossed lovers; it depicts a romance between a South Korean heiress who “crash lands” in a demilitarized zone in North Korea and a captain from the North Korean Special Police Force. The series is accredited for its accurate portrayal of North–South Korean relations and portrayal of North Korean accents, words, and life. It has the second highest rating in South Korean cable history.

Fresh Off the Boat

Based on a book of the same name, Fresh Off the Boat is an situational comedy about an Asian-American family who has moved from the suburbs of Washington, DC to Orlando, FL. Fresh Off the Boat illustrates many cultural aspects of the Asian American identity. Originally from Taiwan, the family struggles to fit in with their White suburban neighbors in Central Florida. The show became the first series on U.S. that features an all Asian-American main cast to broadcast over 100 episodes.

Patriot Act

Premiered on Netflix in October 2018, Hasan Minaj is the first Indian-American host of this weekly comedy show. The show explores the modern cultural and political climate with depth and sincerity. With five seasons to date, Patriot Act has won an Emmy for Outstanding Motion design, a Peabody award, and two Webby awards.

All American Girl

The show features a Korean-American family who owns a bookstore in San Francisco. The show focuses on the daughter of the family, whose American attitude often comes into conflict with those of her more traditional parents. It is the first prime time American television series with the first Asian-American actress leading and the first American TV sitcom featuring an Asian-American family as its focus.

Kim's Convenience

Kim's Convenience is a Netflix show that challenges the existing stereotypes about Asians and portrays a more nuanced picture of the Asian community in North America. This show tells the story of a South Korean family who owns a convenience store in Toronto, Canada. Instead of showing how an Asian family tries to fit in, this show depicts how their community adapts to their Korean idiosyncrasies.

Hawaii Five-O

The series is about a small, specialized task force in Hawaii—headed by Lt. Commander Steve McGarrett, U.S. Navy Reserve. The task force answers only to Hawaii’s governor and investigates crimes ranging from terrorism to kidnapping as well as murder and robberies. The series features two cast members who are of Samoan descent.
